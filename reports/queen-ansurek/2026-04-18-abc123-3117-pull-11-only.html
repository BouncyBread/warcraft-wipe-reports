Mechanic glossary

Light Burn
absorb/heal dot
Heavy heal absorb with ticking damage until fully healed. This is a recovery check, not usually the first mistake: it becomes lethal fast if the target already took avoidable damage or healing falls behind.
How to handle: Top the target quickly, use externals if the burn overlaps other raid damage, and do not let earlier mechanics put the player into the burn already low.
Void Burn
absorb/heal dot
Void-side version of the same heal-absorb burn. Like Light Burn, it usually finishes players who were already behind on health or recovery, rather than being the root cause by itself.
How to handle: Treat it like a recovery check: stabilize the target, clear the absorb, and get them out of any extra overlap damage before the burn ramps.
Light Eruption
interrupt raid-wide
Light-side eruption cast that should be interrupted by Light Feather players. If it goes off, the raid takes a burst of Holy damage.
How to handle: Assign a Light Feather interrupter before the cast starts and make sure they are in range and ready to kick on time.
Void Eruption
interrupt raid-wide
Void-side eruption cast that should be interrupted by Void Feather players. A missed interrupt usually means side assignment or timing broke down.
How to handle: Pre-assign a Void Feather interrupter, keep them on the correct side, and treat the cast as a hard stop rather than a healable event.
Void Edict
frontal knockback
Cone attack in front of the boss. The right person has to take the hit in the correct place or Belo'ren is empowered.
How to handle: Put the assigned player in the cone, aim it away from the raid, and keep everyone else out of the front arc.
Light Edict
frontal knockback
Light-side cone attack in front of the boss. Like Void Edict, it is an assignment mechanic, not a pure healer check.
How to handle: Have the correct Light-side player take the cone, then keep the raid clear of the frontal line and follow-up knockback path.
Voidlight Rupture
soak/spacing assignment
Convergence detonation that punishes bad pairing, spacing, or wrong-side handling.
How to handle: Treat this as the end of the convergence step: pair correctly, keep spacing clean, and do not clear it in a clump.
Light Patch
ground damage-over-time
Lingering light ground effect that ramps damage and punishes bad movement.
How to handle: Move out immediately and do not route later mechanics through it.
Void Patch
ground damage-over-time
Lingering void ground effect that ramps damage and punishes bad movement.
How to handle: Clear the area fast and keep the rest of the raid from having to cross it later.
Burning Heart
raid-wide damage-over-time
Sustained ambient raid damage. It is healable by itself, but it makes every other mechanic harder to recover.
How to handle: Plan healing cooldowns around the overlap windows and avoid letting players enter it already low.
